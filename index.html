FACTS ABOUT CAMEL:-
1.There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
2.Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
3.Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.
4.Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.
5.Thanks to thick pads of skin on their chest and knees, camels can comfortably sit in very hot sand.




FACTS ABOUT CHAMELEON:-
1.Their feet works like salad tongs.
2.Almost half of all known species live in madagascar.
3.Chameleons vary widely in terms of size.
4.They mainly change color in order to regulate or maintain body temperature.
5.Skin crystals enable them to change color at will

FACTS ABOUT PANDA:-
1.Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
2.Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).
3.Pandas have so many fans because they look cute.
4.Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.
5.An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!




FACTS ABOUT PIGEONS:-
1.Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
2.Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
3.Pigeons are highly sociable animals.
4.Pigeons mate for life, and tend to raise two chicks at the same time.
5.Both female and male pigeons share responsibility of caring for and raising young.




FACTS ABOUT TEDDY:-
1.The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
2.Bear hugs predate teddy bears by 60 years. The term bear-hug was first recorded in 1846.
3.The story of Goldilocks And The Three Bears was written in 1837 by Robert Southey who was the Poet Laureate at the time.
4.The Teddy Bears’ Picnic song was originally called The Teddy Bear Two Step.
5. In 1902 in Germany, Steiff launched “Bear 55 PB”, the first toy bear with jointed arms and legs.
